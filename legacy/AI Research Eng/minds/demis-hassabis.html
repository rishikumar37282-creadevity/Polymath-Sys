Nobel Prize in Chemistry (2024). Sir Demis Hassabis is a child chess prodigy, world-class game designer, and neuroscientist who co-founded DeepMind.

"We want to solve intelligence, and then use that to solve everything else."
The Biography

Demis Hassabis is the rarest of polymaths. A child chess master, he founded Elixir Studios at 22 before earning a PhD in Cognitive Neuroscience. This expertise in both the artificial and the biological led to the founding of DeepMind in London in 2010.

DeepMind's mission was clear: combine machine learning and systems neuroscience into a powerful general-purpose learning algorithm. Under his leadership, the lab achieved historic milestones like AlphaGo and AlphaFold.

Hassabis's work on AlphaFold solved one of biology's greatest challenges—predicting the 3D structure of proteins—leading to his 2024 Nobel Prize and cementing AI as an indispensable tool for scientific discovery.

Core Contributions
  • 01
    AlphaFold & Biology
    Solves the protein folding problem, providing the structural shapes of almost all known proteins to the scientific community for free.
  • 02
    AlphaGo & Monte Carlo Tree Search
    Developed the first system to defeat a human world champion at the game of Go, combining Deep RL with advanced tree search.
  • 03
    Neural Turing Machines
    Proposed architectures that couple neural networks to external memory resources, allowing them to learn algorithmic tasks like humans.
