# Math Spine — the non-negotiable daily habit
Runs all 6 months · 30 min/day
This is the thread that connects every domain you want to master. 30 min every morning before anything else. In 6 months at 30 min/day you accumulate 90+ hours of pure math — more than most engineering graduates get in their entire degree.

## Step 1: Linear Algebra — visual intuition first
Months 1–2 · ~20 hours

### Resources
- **3Blue1Brown — Essence of Linear Algebra**
  [YouTube](https://www.youtube.com/playlist?list=PLZHQObOWTQDPD3MizzM2xVFitgF8hE_ab)
  16 videos, ~3 hrs total. The single best visual intuition. Use it twice: once for feeling, once taking notes.
  Tags: #Free #Must-do #YouTube
  
- **MIT 18.06 — Gilbert Strang (OCW)**
  [Link](https://ocw.mit.edu/courses/18-06-linear-algebra-spring-2010/)
  After 3B1B, Strang gives you rigor. Focus on LU, QR, SVD.
  Tags: #Free #Core #MIT-OCW

- **Strang's "Introduction to Linear Algebra" — selected chapters**
  Free PDF legally available via MIT OCW. Focus on: Ch1–4, Ch6, Ch7.
  Tags: #Free-PDF #Textbook

**What you actually do:** Day 1 — open 3B1B Ep1 (9 min). Day 2 — Ep2. After all 16 episodes (3 weeks), switch to Strang lectures. Solve 3–5 problems per lecture. Do not move to probability until you can compute an SVD by hand.

## Step 2: Probability & Statistics — the language of ML
Months 2–4 · ~25 hours

### Resources
- **Harvard Stat110 — Joe Blitzstein (YouTube + edX)**
  [YouTube](https://www.youtube.com/playlist?list=PL2SOU6wwxB0uwwH80KTQ6ht66KWxbzTIo)
  Best probability course ever. Blitzstein's story-based teaching style.
  Tags: #Free #Must-do #YouTube

- **StatQuest with Josh Starmer**
  [YouTube](https://www.youtube.com/@statquest)
  Best visual explanations for p-values, Bayes, hypothesis testing, etc. 
  Tags: #Free #YouTube

- **Think Bayes + Think Stats — Allen Downey**
  [Free online](https://greenteapress.com/wp/)
  Teaches through Python simulation.
  Tags: #Free #Book #Python
