How the greats actually thought
The polymath mental operating system
Feynman, Von Neumann, and Nash didn't have more hours. They had a different relationship with confusion, depth, and first principles.
Feynman technique
Teach it to a child
After every concept: write it in plain English Where you get stuck = your gap Go back, fill the gap, rewrite Apply to every math + ML concept you study
Von Neumann method
Ruthless formalization
For every concept: write the formal definition Find the edge cases and boundary conditions Ask: how does this connect to what I already know? Build a personal "concept graph" in Obsidian
Hassabis method
Games as testbeds
Build toy versions of hard problems Measure everything — treat learning as an experiment Cross-pollinate: chess thinking → optimization → AI Every paper you read: reproduce the key result
The connecting thread across all domains
The Riemann Hypothesis, Quantum Mechanics, Deep Learning, and Game Theory all share one underlying language: linear operators on function spaces (functional analysis). This is the meta-subject that unifies them. You don't need to study it directly now — but every time you study any of these fields, ask: "what is the operator here, what space is it acting on, what are the eigenvalues?" This question alone will give you 80% of the insight that took others years to discover.
