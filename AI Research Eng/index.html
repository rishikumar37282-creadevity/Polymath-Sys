Why This Exists

The Cure for Optimal Strategy Paralysis

Optimal Strategy Paralysis is the specific trap that ambitious, high-intelligence people fall into — and it has a precise mechanism worth understanding before the cure makes sense.

The mechanism first. OSP is not about laziness or lack of discipline — it's a rational response to a real cognitive problem that then becomes a trap. Here's how it works:

When the number of possible paths is large and the cost of choosing the wrong one feels high, your brain does something logical: it delays commitment and gathers more information. This feels productive. You're researching, comparing, planning. But the information never becomes complete enough to feel safe, so the loop continues. Planning becomes the activity instead of the means to an activity.

The cruel irony: the more intelligent and ambitious you are, the worse this gets. You can genuinely see more paths, imagine more failure modes, and generate more "what if I'm missing something" scenarios. Your intelligence, which should be an asset, becomes the engine of your own paralysis.
