Fine-tuning — when and how to adapt foundation models
Weeks 10–12 of this track
▼
▶
HuggingFace NLP Course — huggingface.co/learn/nlp-course PrimaryFree
Completely free. The definitive guide to the HuggingFace ecosystem: tokenizers, datasets, Trainer API, PEFT, LoRA fine-tuning. Chapters 1–4 are essential. Chapter 7 (question answering + summarization fine-tuning) is directly applicable to your RAG projects. Run every notebook in Colab.
▶
LoRA + QLoRA papers + Tim Dettmers blog Free
LoRA paper (arxiv.org/abs/2106.09685) + QLoRA paper (arxiv.org/abs/2305.14314). Tim Dettmers' blog (timdettmers.com) is the clearest explanation of quantization and efficient fine-tuning. Together these give you the theory to fine-tune a 7B model on a free Colab T4 GPU — which you will do as a project.
Fine-tuning vs RAG decision framework: Use RAG when the knowledge changes frequently or is too large to fit in context. Use fine-tuning when you need to change the model's behavior, style, or reasoning pattern — not just its knowledge. Most production systems use both: fine-tuning for behavior, RAG for knowledge. Being able to articulate this distinction is worth ₹10L in consulting value per conversation.

M3
Linear algebra — advanced topics connecting to quantum
Months 18–24 · planting the Phase 3 seed
▼
📖
Linear Algebra Done Right — Sheldon Axler Primary BookBook · Free 4th ed. PDF
Free PDF of 4th edition at linear.axler.net. Axler builds linear algebra on linear maps rather than matrices — the approach used in quantum mechanics and functional analysis. This is the bridge book between Phase 2 linear algebra and Phase 3 quantum computing. Every theorem feels inevitable rather than arbitrary. Read in the final 6 months of Phase 2 as you prepare for the quantum track.
The connecting insight for Phase 3: Quantum states are vectors in a complex Hilbert space. Quantum operations are unitary linear operators. Measurement is projection onto an eigenspace. Entanglement is a tensor product structure. If you understand Axler's linear algebra, you already understand 70% of the mathematics of quantum computing — you just need to extend it to complex vector spaces. This is why the Phase 2 math investment pays off in Phase 3.
