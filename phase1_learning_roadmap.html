Math Spine — the non-negotiable daily habit
This is the thread that connects every domain you want to master. 30 min every morning before anything else. In 6 months at 30 min/day you accumulate 90+ hours of pure math — more than most engineering graduates get in their entire degree.

Full MIT course: lectures + problem sets + exams, all free. After 3B1B gives you intuition, Strang gives you rigor. Focus on: vector spaces, matrix factorizations (LU, QR, SVD). SVD alone is worth the entire course — it underpins PCA, recommenders, and compression.

Probability & Statistics — the language of ML
Months 2–4 · ~25 hours
▼
▶
Harvard Stat110 — Joe Blitzstein (YouTube + edX)
Widely considered the best probability course ever recorded. Free on YouTube. Covers: conditional probability, distributions, expectation, CLT, Markov chains. Blitzstein's story-based teaching style makes abstract concepts visceral. The Strategic Practice problems are essential — do every one.
FreeMust-doYouTube
📖
StatQuest with Josh Starmer
YouTube channel. Best visual explanations of: p-values, Bayes theorem, distributions, hypothesis testing, and later: PCA, regression, random forests. Use alongside Stat110 to reinforce every concept visually. Subscribe and watch in parallel with your study sessions.
FreeYouTube
📖
Think Bayes + Think Stats — Allen Downey (free online)
Both books are free at greenteapress.com. Downey teaches probability and stats through Python simulation — perfect for you as a programmer. Reading Think Bayes will make Bayesian ML intuitive when you reach it in phase 2.
FreeBookPython
The key insight: Every ML algorithm is a probability statement. Logistic regression = conditional probability. Naive Bayes = Bayes theorem. Neural networks = function approximators trained by minimizing expected loss. Learn probability and ML theory becomes obvious instead of mysterious.

Mathematics for Machine Learning — Deisenroth et al. (free PDF)
Free at mml-book.github.io. The definitive book connecting math to ML. Covers linear algebra, calculus, probability, and optimization all in one place. Don't read linearly — use as a reference when you hit a concept in your ML course that you don't understand mathematically.
Free PDFReference bibleBook
For quantum physics later: When you reach Phase 3, you'll need complex numbers, Fourier transforms, and linear operators. Plant this seed now — whenever you study eigenvectors, note that quantum states ARE eigenvectors of the Hamiltonian operator. The math is identical. Physics just gives the variables different names.

Months 3–4 · Core ML theory + practice
Classical ML — where your DA background becomes a superpower
This is where most people rush through to get to deep learning. Don't. Classical ML — regression, trees, ensemble methods — is what runs 80% of production ML systems. It is also what every ML interview tests first.
1

6–8 weeks · primary resource
▼
▶
Andrew Ng — ML Specialization (Coursera — audit free)
3-course specialization. Audit it free — all video + quizzes available without paying; only graded assignments require payment. Course 1: Supervised learning (regression, classification, regularization). Course 2: Advanced algorithms (trees, ensembles, neural nets intro). Course 3: Unsupervised + recommenders. This is still the gold standard introduction despite being updated — Ng's explanations of gradient descent and cost functions are unmatched.

Months 4–5 · Neural networks to transformers
Deep Learning — where the modern AI era lives
Do not start this track until you can explain gradient descent, cross-entropy loss, and backpropagation without notes. Those three concepts ARE deep learning — everything else is architecture choices built on top of them.

The "makemore" and "nanoGPT" videos are essential.

Free on all platforms. Create one card per concept you study — definition on front, Feynman explanation + example on back. 15 min of Anki review per day retains more than 2 hours of re-reading notes. Use for: math definitions, ML algorithm intuitions, DSA complexity, Java API methods.

Free personal knowledge management. Create a note per concept, link related concepts with [[double brackets]]. Your personal "concept graph" connecting math, ML, Java, system design. In 6 months you'll have a searchable second brain. The graph view showing connected concepts is deeply motivating.
FreePKM
✅
Notion (free tier) — weekly review template
Every Sunday: 15 minutes answering 3 questions. (1) What did I complete this week? (2) What confused me most — and did I resolve it? (3) What is the single most important thing to do next week? This weekly review is what separates people who compound from people who stay busy.
Free tierReview system
The Feynman test — run it weekly: Pick any concept you studied this week. Open a blank document. Explain it in plain English as if teaching a smart 16-year-old with no background. Where you get stuck or vague = your actual gap. Go back, fill the gap, rewrite. Every polymath on your list used this exact process. Feynman called it his only technique. It works because it makes the gap visible instead of hidden.
